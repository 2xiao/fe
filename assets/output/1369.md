## 题目

Table: `UserActivity`

> 
> 
> 
> 
> 
> +---------------+---------+
> 
> | Column Name   | Type> 
> |
> 
> +---------------+---------+
> 
> | username> 
>   | varchar |
> 
> | activity> 
>   | varchar |
> 
> | startDate> 
>  | Date> 
> |
> 
> | endDate> 
>    | Date> 
> |
> 
> +---------------+---------+
> 
> This table may contain duplicates rows.
> 
> This table contains information about the activity performed by each user in a period of time.
> 
> A person with username performed an activity from startDate to endDate.
> 
> 



Write a solution to show the **second most recent activity** of each user.

If the user only has one activity, return that one. A user cannot perform more
than one activity at the same time.

Return the result table in **any** order.

The result format is in the following example.



**Example 1:**

> Input: 
> 
> UserActivity table:
> 
> +------------+--------------+-------------+-------------+
> 
> | username   | activity> 
>  | startDate   | endDate> 
>  |
> 
> +------------+--------------+-------------+-------------+
> 
> | Alice> 
>   | Travel> 
>    | 2020-02-12  | 2020-02-20  |
> 
> | Alice> 
>   | Dancing> 
>   | 2020-02-21  | 2020-02-23  |
> 
> | Alice> 
>   | Travel> 
>    | 2020-02-24  | 2020-02-28  |
> 
> | Bob> 
> > 
> | Travel> 
>    | 2020-02-11  | 2020-02-18  |
> 
> +------------+--------------+-------------+-------------+
> 
> Output: 
> 
> +------------+--------------+-------------+-------------+
> 
> | username   | activity> 
>  | startDate   | endDate> 
>  |
> 
> +------------+--------------+-------------+-------------+
> 
> | Alice> 
>   | Dancing> 
>   | 2020-02-21  | 2020-02-23  |
> 
> | Bob> 
> > 
> | Travel> 
>    | 2020-02-11  | 2020-02-18  |
> 
> +------------+--------------+-------------+-------------+
> 
> Explanation: 
> 
> The most recent activity of Alice is Travel from 2020-02-24 to 2020-02-28, before that she was dancing from 2020-02-21 to 2020-02-23.
> 
> Bob only has one record, we just take that one.
> 
>
